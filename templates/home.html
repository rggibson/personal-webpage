Welcome to my web page! My name is Richard Gibson and I live in Victoria, BC, Canada. I currently work remotely with a small group of professional poker players as a computer programmer and consultant to help improve their play. Some aspects of my work include building game-theoretic optimal computer poker programs, hosting them on a private online poker "training" room and developing custom software for analyzing the programs' strategies.

I recently completed my Ph.D. in Computing Science at the University of Alberta. As a member of the Computer Poker Research Group, the focus of my Ph.D. thesis is in artificial intelligence and computational game theory, specifically in domains involving more than two agents. From 2010 to 2013, I was the main author of Hyperborean3p, a program that plays 3-player limit Texas hold'em. Hyperborean3p has won the 3-player events at the Annual Computer Poker Competition for five years running and is arguably the best 3-player poker program in the world.

Before engaging in Computing Science, I studied Mathematics and hold a Master's degree in Math from Simon Fraser University. In general, I am interested in fun and challenging problems where I can utilize both my computing and mathematical expertise. I am particularly interested in pursuing:

  • Research in Artificial Intelligence and Games
  • Video Game Development
  • Tech Startups

Outside of work and studying, I enjoy playing several sports, including tennis, baseball and ball hockey. Furthermore, I am an avid video game player and love playing games both casually and competitively. Finally, I'm a husband to my wife Melissa and dad to my son Jack who was born in 2013.
