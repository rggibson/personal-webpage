Welcome to my web page! My name is Richard Gibson and I live in Victoria, BC, Canada. I recently completed my Ph.D. in Computing Science at the University of Alberta. As a member of the Computer Poker Research Group, the focus of my Ph.D. thesis is in artificial intelligence and computational game theory, specifically in domains involving more than two agents. From 2010 to 2013, I was the main author of Hyperborean3p, a program that plays 3-player limit Texas hold'em. Hyperborean3p won the 3-player events at the 2013 Annual Computer Poker Competition and is arguably the best 3-player poker program in the world. I am now lined up to work as a poker AI consultant for a small group of pro poker players who wish to use poker AI as a training partner.

Before engaging in Computing Science, I studied Mathematics and hold a Master's degree in Math from Simon Fraser University. In general, I am interested in fun and challenging problems where I can utilize both my computing and mathematical expertise. I am particularly interested in pursuing:

  • Research in Artificial Intelligence
  • Video Game Development
  • Tech Startups

Outside of work and studying, I enjoy playing several sports, including tennis, baseball and ball hockey. Furthermore, I am an avid video game player and love playing games both casually and competively. In particular, I often attempt speedruns of some of my favourite games where the goal is to finish the game as fast as possible. Most of the games I play are from classic consoles such as the original Nintendo and the Sega Genesis. I go by rggibson in the speedrunning community and upload several of my runs to YouTube.
